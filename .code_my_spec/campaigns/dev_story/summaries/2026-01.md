# January 2026: The First Spark

MetricFlow came to life on the last night of January 2026. In a single focused evening session, the entire foundation of a marketing analytics platform was laid down in three rapid commits.

It started the way all Phoenix projects do -- with `mix phx.new`. The initial commit landed a fully configured Elixir/Phoenix application, complete with DaisyUI theming, Heroicons, a topbar progress indicator, and the standard scaffolding of configs, endpoints, and error handlers. Over four thousand lines of code hit the repository in one shot, establishing the build system, asset pipeline, and deployment configurations that everything else would grow from.

Minutes later, the second commit doubled down with `phx.gen.auth`, wiring up a complete authentication system. Users, tokens, sessions, registration, login, confirmation, and settings pages all materialized at once -- nearly 3,300 lines across 29 files. The `Scope` pattern was introduced from the start, signaling an intent toward multi-tenant architecture. A comprehensive test suite accompanied every module, setting a tone of disciplined coverage that would carry forward through the project's life.

The final commit of the month was small but deliberate: documentation was linked in as a git submodule, keeping the codebase and its reference material connected but cleanly separated.

Three commits. One evening. Over 7,400 lines of working, tested code. The pace was unmistakable -- this was not a tentative experiment but a builder who knew exactly what they wanted to create and moved with conviction. By midnight, MetricFlow had authentication, a design system, and a solid architectural skeleton. The foundation was set for everything that would follow.
