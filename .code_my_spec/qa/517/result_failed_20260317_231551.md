## Issues

### Sync schedule description does not mention Google Business Profile

#### Severity
MEDIUM

#### Scope
APP

#### Description
The `[data-role="sync-schedule"]` section in `SyncHistory` contains a static description paragraph that lists covered providers as "Google Ads, Facebook Ads, Google Analytics" (marketing) and "QuickBooks" (financial). Google Business Profile is not listed, even though the story 517 implementation adds a Google Business Profile sync worker.

Affected file: `lib/metric_flow_web/live/integration_live/sync_history.ex`, lines 49–53.

The description must be updated to include Google Business Profile (and Google Search Console, which was added in story 516). The BDD spec `criterion_4819` has a scenario "Google Business Profile appears in the sync schedule section as a covered provider" that asserts this text is present and currently fails.

To reproduce: navigate to `http://localhost:4070/integrations/sync-history` and inspect the schedule card. The text "Google Business Profile" does not appear.

### google_business and google_business_reviews absent from @provider_names map

#### Severity
LOW

#### Scope
APP

#### Description
`SyncHistory.@provider_names` (line 20 of `sync_history.ex`) lists explicit display names for `google_ads`, `facebook_ads`, `google_analytics`, `google_search_console`, `quickbooks`, and `google`. The `:google_business` and `:google_business_reviews` providers are absent.

These providers fall through to `derive_display_name/1`, which produces "Google Business" and "Google Business Reviews" respectively. The derived names are acceptable, but the explicit map is the canonical place for display names and the omission is inconsistent with how other providers are registered. Adding them to `@provider_names` would make the intent explicit and ensure future renames are handled in one place.

No user-visible rendering failure occurs — the derived names display correctly. This is a code quality gap rather than a functional bug.
